What the sweep tells us
Three honest readings:

There is a marginally positive slice. Tier A at threshold 0.80 gives ROI +2.6% over n=72, with win rate 51.4%, Brier 0.2652 (better than Tier B's 0.2696). It beats Tier B on win rate, ROI, and Brier simultaneously.

The signal is not "sharper at the top." Brier stays in a tight 0.2650–0.2656 band from threshold 0.65 through 0.80 — calibration does not improve as we tighten. The ROI gain comes from filtering out a roughly-balanced subset of losers, not from finding a sharper sub-population. At 0.85 Brier worsens (0.2829) — a sign the very top of the rank-score distribution is mostly variance, not skill.

Win rate is still below the −110 breakeven (52.4%) at every threshold. Positive ROI at 0.80 (+2.6%) is achievable below breakeven only because non-pick'em odds vary; it is not driven by the model picking winners more often than coinflip + vig. That is the definition of fragile.
